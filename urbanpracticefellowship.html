Project Statement

Below is a list of community partners that are willing to host a fellow in 2016. Please select the community partner that interests you most and tailor your statement as if you were applying directly to that organization. Your statement should demonstrate that you have researched the organization, and that your project reasonably aligns with its work and mission, as well as any additional description included below. NOTE: You should not contact the community partner directly.

Berlin

  • Environmental Justice Institute – Fellows will research and write blog posts, attend a graduate-level course in climate justice and governance, and collaborate with Berlin-based graduate students working on the social impact of climate change.
  • Center for Artistic Activism – Fellows will be conducting highly independent, ethnographic research to determine the efficacy of creative activism in Berlin.

Madrid

  • SEDOAC – Fellows will work with the organization to build the domestic workers’ rights campaign.
  • Paisaje Transversal – Fellows will work with the organization to identify a participatory design project appropriate to the fellow’s interests and skills.
  • MediaLab Prado) – Fellows will produce digital projects that support social justice and citizenship-building initiatives in Madrid.

New York

  • The Laundromat Project – Fellows will work with artists and program staff to produce collaborative artworks that are inspired by and created for the community in which the work is produced.
  • Southside United HDFC (Los Sures) – Fellows will assist the staff at Los Sures in both quantitatively and qualitatively researching the impact of displacement and gentrification on this South Williamsburg, Brooklyn community.
  • Bronx Music Heritage Center – Fellows will be producing interviews and research on the musical heritage of Bronx-based Albanian, Bangladeshi, Yemeni and other immigrant groups.

Buenos Aires

  • Center for Artistic Activism – Fellows will be conducting highly independent, ethnographic research to determine the efficacy of creative activism in Buenos Aires.
  • La Juanita – Fellows will collaborate with members of this educational and labor cooperative to identify projects most useful to the community.

Please attach a 2-3 page Project Statement that answers the following:

  • What research do you intend to conduct through this fellowship and what research have you already completed on this subject? Keep in mind that the work you do with the community partner may also involve research that is relevant to you, but here we are asking you to tell us about your own research project and how it might be served by the fellowship

Recommendation Letters

Please submit two letters of recommendation, one from your adviser and one from a faculty member who can speak to your academic strengths; knowledge of urban theory, research, or history, if any; and your ability to be a self-starter.

Letters may be emailed as an attachment to urbandemos@nyu.edu (.doc, .docx, or .pdf format) or mailed/delivered to the Gallatin Office of Civic Engagement, 411 Lafayette Street, 3rd Floor, New York, NY 10003.
