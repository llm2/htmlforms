This study plan is designed to help you and your adviser define your educational goals and to identify strategies for accomplishing them. Continuing students should answer all four questions below; entering students should answer the first two.


PROGRESS TOWARD THE MASTER'S DEGREE

This worksheet is designed to remind you and your adviser of your degree requirements and to review your progress toward the Master's degree. Students should consult the Gallatin Bulletin or http://www.gallatin.nyu.edu/academics/graduate/requirements.html for a full description of degree requirements and restrictions.

DEGREE REQUIREMENT

1. TOTAL UNITS: The Master's Degree requires the completion of 40 units.


2. GRADE POINT AVERAGE: You must maintain a minimum grade point average of 3.00 to remain in good academic standing; a final minimum cumulative grade point average of 3.00 is required for graduation.


3. CLASSROOM COURSES: You are required to complete a minimum of 14 units in classroom courses. This does not include the Graduate Core or individualized projects such as Independent Study, Tutorial, Internship, or Private Lessons.


4. GRADUATE CORE: You are required to take the following Gallatin courses, totaling 14 units.


5. MASTER'S THESIS: You must complete and successfully defend a master's thesis in order to graduate. For details about the thesis, please consult the Gallatin Web site: http://www.gallatin.nyu.edu/academics/graduate/thesis.html


DEGREE RESTRICTIONS


TRANSFER AND COURSE EQUIVALENCY CREDIT: You may be granted up to a combined total of 12 units in transfer and course equivalency. These units must be for courses generally related to your area of concentration. If course equivalency units are awarded, they will be applied equally to the classroom unit requirement (14-unit minimum) and individualized project units (12-unit maximum); they also count toward the 28-unit residency requirement. Transfer units count toward the classroom unit requirement.

TIME LIMIT: You are allowed a maximum of 6 years and a minimum of 3 semesters for completion of the Master's Degree.

STUDENT INFORMATION


REGISTRATION RESTRICTIONS:


COURSE LEVELS: Only graduate-level courses will count for credit toward the master's degree. The only exception is the Department of Film and Television in the Tisch School of the Arts, where Gallatin students are permitted to take a maximum of 12 undergraduate units. The undergraduate courses in this department must be at least at the 1000 level, meaning the catalog number must be at least four digits and begin with the number 1, such as FMTV-UT 1060. No undergraduate film course at the 0000 level (where the catalog number is less than four digits, or begins with a 0) will count for credit towards the Gallatin M.A. degree.


INDIVIDUALIZED PROJECTS: You may take a maximum of 12 units in individualized courses: Independent Study, Tutorial, Internship, and Private Lessons. The required independent study, Review of the Literature, is not counted in these 12 units.


STERN SCHOOL OF BUSINESS: Students may take a maximum of 6 units per semester in courses offered by the Stern School of Business.


MAINTAINING MATRICULATION: You may maintain matriculation by fee for a maximum of 2 semesters.
