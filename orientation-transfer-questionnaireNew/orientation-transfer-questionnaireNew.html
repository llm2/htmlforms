What kinds of courses do you plan to take and in which NYU schools and departments ( e.g. the Marketing Department, Stern School of Business, Steinhardt School of Education, the Sociology Department, etc.) are you interested?

Non-classroom study in the form of internships, independent studies, private lessons, and tutorials can constitute a significant part of your concentration. Please tell us which of these non-classroom study opportunities you plan to integrate into your program of study.

Do you plan to study abroad? If so, which term and where? (NOTE: Transfer students are not permitted to study abroad in their first semester at Gallatin.)

The Office of Advising offers walk-in hours for general questions regarding Gallatin policies and procedures. As a Gallatin student, you will be assigned to a primary faculty adviser whose academic interests match yours. Students and advisers work together to map out the concentration and each semester's specific plan of study. Keeping this in mind, what academic training, scholarly or professional interests, and background do you most desire from a primary adviser?
